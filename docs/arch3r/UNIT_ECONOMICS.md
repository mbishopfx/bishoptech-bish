# ARCH3R Unit Economics

This document gives a practical current-state economics framing using the pricing and bundled AI budgets already encoded in the product.

## Important note

These numbers are **gross contribution baselines**, not final net profit.

They intentionally focus on:

- subscription revenue
- bundled AI budget
- contribution before infra, support, labor, and provider-specific campaign costs

## Current core plans

### Starter

- Price: `$499/mo`
- Bundled AI budget: `$50/mo`
- Baseline gross contribution before infra/support/labor: `$449/mo`

### Growth

- Price: `$1,499/mo`
- Bundled AI budget: `$250/mo`
- Baseline gross contribution before infra/support/labor: `$1,249/mo`

### Business

- Price: `$3,500/mo`
- Bundled AI budget: `$1,000/mo`
- Baseline gross contribution before infra/support/labor: `$2,500/mo`

### Enterprise

- Custom pricing
- Custom AI budget or BYOK mix
- Gross contribution depends on negotiated contract shape

## Additional revenue levers

These are upside, not baseline margin:

- setup fees
- additional seats
- plugin add-ons
- premium support or onboarding
- managed campaign/runtime services
- enterprise customization

## Why the economics model is attractive

ARCH3R is not a single-surface chat tool. One org can monetize across:

- core collaboration value
- AI usage
- seat expansion
- add-on plugins
- managed runtime services
- high-touch enterprise setup

That creates more revenue depth per customer than a simple per-seat AI chatbot.

## Why plugin activation matters financially

The marketplace model creates cleaner packaging:

- low-friction base platform sale
- optional upsell into social
- optional upsell into voice
- optional upsell into SMS
- future upsell into additional operational plugins

## Economics caveats

True net profitability still depends on:

- infra hosting cost
- support load
- model/provider mix
- Vapi/Twilio/social provider costs
- file storage and sync usage
- customer success and onboarding time

For investor and sales discussions, the safest current phrasing is:

> ARCH3R already has visible gross contribution per org at the subscription layer, and the plugin/runtime model creates expansion revenue beyond the base workspace.
